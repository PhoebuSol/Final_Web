Sony Has No Plans to Copy Game Pass

On today's Daily Fix, Daemon discusses Sony's plan to keep exclusives off PS PS Plus at lunch, the story behind the Yakuza game's confusing name change, and CD Projekt Red is about to start work on Cyberpunk 2. PS Now predated Game Pass. Not an argument either way. Just a clarification of facts. And I think that the Plus strategy is better, overall, for the industry. Keep the subscription as a supplement to a library, not the primary.

The Last of Us Is Getting a Universal Halloween Nights Experience

Universal is teaming up with PlayStation to create a haunted house experience based on Naughty Dog's The Last of Us, which will feature iconic locations from the 2013 game, clickers, and more as part of its Halloween Horror Nights series of in-person events. The themed experiences will see guests follow in the footsteps of series protagonists Joel and Ellie as they encounter with enemies and visit known locations

Phantom Liberty Director Will Lead Cyberpunk 2077 Sequel

Director of Cyberpunk 2077's Phantom Liberty expansion Gabe Amatangelo will also direct the full sequel from CD Projekt Red. Speaking to Bloomberg, Amatangelo said he'll be moving onto the sequel once development on the long-awaited Cyberpunk 2077 expansion is complete. Given Phantom Liberty launches on September 26, this should be within the next three months or so.

Lies of P Modders Immediately Turn Demo Into Bloodborne on PC

FromSoftware fans are officially done waiting for Bloodborne on PC and as a result have made their own version out of the recently released Lies of P demo. Anyone familiar with the likes of Elden Ring and Dark Souls will immediately notice similarities between Lies of P and FromSoftware's library of RPGs, Bloodborne in particular.

First Star Wars: Outlaws Gameplay Shows Firefights, Space Battles, and Branching Dialogue

Following the announcement of Star Wars: Outlaws at the Xbox Showcase, Ubisoft revealed the first gameplay trailer at Ubisoft Forward. The nearly 10-minute clip showed off everything from combat to space dogfights in what’s looking to be an expansive, open-world adventure.

Assassin’s Creed Codename Jade Enters Public Testing Later This Month

Sandwiched between Nexus and Mirage at today's Ubisoft Forward event, we got a longer look at the upcoming mobile Assassin's Creed Codename Jade, which is preparing to enter a public testing phase ahead of launch. Codename Jade is being made in partnership with Tencent's Level Infinite publishing division, and is a mobile-first Assassin's Creed game that takes place in China.
